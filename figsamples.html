A Sampler of FIGlet Font Conversions and Designs by Kent Nassen

There are 19 FIGlet fonts shown here. Below, I've tried to give credit where credit is due for font designs. Also see the full font listings since I usually put full credit (Usenet posting, etc.) in the comments at the beginning of each of my fonts. No guarantees on these e-mail addresses; some of them are several years old by now. All of these FIGlet fonts were assembled/ constructed/converted by me, and some are even my own designs.

In naming the fonts, I've tried to go with the designer's wishes, if stated, or in keeping with usual (typesetting) font-naming schemes, stuck the designer's name on it one way or another. If neither of those methods worked, I made something up. In one case I took a poll on a local BBS system (and that's how tombstone.flf got its name).

Bell This font is based on the upper case letters designed by Joshua Bell (jsbell@acs.ucalgary.ca). I've added the lower case letters and punctuation, plus a few touch-ups along the way to make things work a bit better with FIGlet.

SmKeyBoard I liked this one so much, I've been using for my .signature for several years (which found its way into Sven Gucke's net .sig collection). The design credit goes to Bas Meijer (bas@damek.kth.se), who posted one of the more interesting newsgroup comments I've seen in rec.arts.ascii (see the comments in the full font). I filled in the rest of the characters (pretty easy, eh?). Try this font with Paul Burton's FigWin program using "vertical smushing" and you can come very close to duplicating Bas Meijer's post...one of these days I'll have to add that Return key, though!
