Kent's Conversions/Designs
These are my designs/conversions and conversions of other people's designs that I've contributed to the FIGlet archives. I'm sort of on hiatus right now, but I've got several partially-finished conversions and designs waiting for completion. Maybe someday... I have a few of the fonts I've contributed available to look at here in a sampler and as full FIGlet font files to view or download. Maybe I'll add more later.

Other FIGlet Fonts
Here is a relatively complete collection of the rest of the contributed and default FIGfonts (actually this is grex's figlet fonts directory, so some of these may not exactly match those in the official FIGlet distribution and contributed fonts collections; I've made a few corrections here and there, or added some characters at the request of grex users in other words).

FIGlet Home Page (Ian Chai)
Answers the questions "What is FIGlet?" "Why does FIGlet exist?" and "Where do I get FIGlet?" This is the main site for all FIGlet activities, including standard and contributed fonts, as well as binaries for several platforms. Ian is the "I" in FIGlet.

New Official FIGlet FTP site [broken link]
As of 12/9/97 this is the new Official FIGlet FTP site. Thanks to the new providers willing to host the well-known and highly celebrated utility known as FIGlet!

FIGlet FTP site [broken link]
An alternative way of getting fonts, source, and binaries for FIGlet. But not the preferred access method--it can be slow... Use FIGlet Home Page instead, if you can.

Paul Burton's FigWin Site [Broken Link]
FigWin is a GUI adaptation of FIGlet which has a number of interesting and unique features compared to the regular command-line FIGlet including vertical smushing and adjustable magnification of fonts on screen. FigWin can also be found at the FIGlet home site in the /pub/figlet/program/windows directory.

FIGlet FAQ
This is an OLD abbreviated FAQ that was posted to the FIGlet mail list. It tells how to subscribe and unsubscribe from the mail list, how to get more fonts, as well as how to reach the main FIGlet site.

FIGlet Mail List Archive [broken link]
An archive of postings to the Figlet-L mail list.

alt.ascii-art
The Usenet group rec.arts.ascii appears to have gone away when its moderator retired, so alt.ascii-art is the remaining main ASCII art news group. Occasionally, people will post fonts here or announce new FIGlet on-line services and software ports, but beware, without moderation, there is a lot of garbage to sort through. Note: as of 2025 getting usenet is still possible, but not as easy as it was 25 years ago. If you have access to Usenet from your ISP and they get alt.ascii-art, take a look (and hope it hasn't reduced to a trickle).

Wicked ASCII Art
A very large ASCII art site. This site has not been updated since 1999, so is a static representation of the original site.
