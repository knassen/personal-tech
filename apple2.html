Apple II Sites and Information

In honor of my very first personal computer, an Apple ][e, here are links to some of the many Apple II sites out there on the web. In my current personal Apple ][ hardware collection are a ROM3 IIgs, a ROM1 IIgs, and Apple //c+, three Apple //e's, an Apple //c, a good ol' Apple II+, and a cool-looking Darth Vader (black, Bell & Howell) II, all of which I still play around on once in a while. The other day I found my Assembly Lines: The Book book...does that ever bring back the memories...wonder what I did with my Red Book? Oh, here it is... they sure don't build and document computers like this any more.
  • Gopher to Umich Apple2 Archive [bad link]
    Also reachable via AFS (/afs/umich.edu/group/itd/archive/apple2) and FTP (apple2.archive.umich.edu). Has programs and information for both 8-bit and 16-bit Apples, as well as Apple Technotes.
  • FTP to apple2.caltech.edu Apple2 Archive (Babylon) [bad link]
    Home of the Caltech Apple II Archive. Programs for 8-bit Apples and the IIgs, source code for Apple2 utility programs, demos, emulators, games, information, and more.
  • FTP to ground.icaen.uiowa.edu Apple2 Archive [bad link]
    People from all over the net donated money to purchase a 1.7 gig hard drive that now houses the ground Apple archive. A2 Genie Lamps, nulib v3.25, Apple II Technical Notes, emulators, Beagle Oldies, games, graphics, languages, Eamon modules, etc. Supports 8-bit and 16-bit Apples.
  • Nathan Mates's (A2) Home Page [bad link]
    Well, this great Apple II resource is now gone...the only thing here now is Nathan's list of reasons why he's leaving the A2 community.
  • FTP to ftp.uu.net /systems/apple2 [bad link]
    A mirror of the Umich Apple II Archive.
  • Apple2 comp.sys.apple2 FAQ [bad link]
    The CSA2 FAQ, plus FAQs for accelerators, Applesoft BASIC, CD-ROMs, Flopticals, and Sound & Music.
  • Apple II Emulation Page [bad link]
    Claims to have tested emulators, selected the best, most usable ones and made them available at this site. (Which appears to be true).
  • Apple II Emulators (Zophar's Domain)
    A list of Apple II emulators with links to further information.
  • Chris, Mark and Terry's Apple II Page [bad link]
    Boot up DOS 3.3 and enter Chris, Mark and Terry's Apple II Page. Much historical information about the Apple II and its hacker culture. Screendumps from more the 500 classic Apple II games and a list of Apple ProDOS commands. And other stuff like more links to the Apple newsgroups, etc.
  • Apple ][ History [bad link]
    Covers all Apple ][ models (what was and what might have been) as well as programming languages, disk operating systems, software, and magazines, among other Apple ][ historical topics.
